Differences between years:
In the year 2014, 2015 and 2016 we can see a general increase of gun violence incidents as the year passes. In contrast, we discovered a general decrease of incidents in the year 2017. The underlying cause is that incidents sky rocketed on average in the end of 2016 and in the beginning of 2017. The year 2016 ended with an average of ±175 incidents per day and on the first day of 2017 there were more incidents than ever, that is to say 342 reported incidents. This caused the average trend line in 2017 to start off very high and it restored slowly to a more ‘normal’ amount of an average of ±160 incidents at the end of 2017.
